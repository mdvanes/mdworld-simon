Abstract

The search for practical applications of intelligent systems still continues. In 1996, Crites published his dissertation concerning the use of reinforcement learning agents in the field of elevator group control. Elevator group control has grown rather more complex over the past decades, and Crites suggested this approach in reply to the existing algorithms. The existing control mechanisms were almost all superseded by his reinforcement learning agent.

On the other hand, architecture has not stopped developing since 1996. I wanted to research more extreme situations in the form of mega-high-rise structures, i.e. skyscrapers surpassing 70 floors. The traffic in such buildings takes such complex routing mechanisms, that much can be optimized by the use of a learning controller. One of the enhancements in the elevator control of mega-high-rise structures is the application of destination control. Instead of an up and a down button in the hall where the passengers arrive, there is a panel with buttons for all the possible destination floors. This way the destination of passengers is known in advance. Elevator systems have shown to profit from this additional information, so I adopt it as one of this research’s basic principles.

As a final challenge, I will program everything related to this research in the programming language Haskell, including the graphical user interface. Haskell is a relatively little used language, but because of its functional nature, it seems excellent for computing with neural networks. Next to nothing has been done with practical implementations of intelligent agents in Haskell, and therefore this study will certainly come up with useful feedback about Haskell’s aptness for this field of programming.

Notes
(1)
Why did I stop researching RPROP:
RPROP promises faster learning speeds than backpropagation. About five times faster, to be more precise. Unfortunately, this only holds for batch-learning. On-line learning backpropagation usually learns 30 times faster than batch-learning. In this research after each step taken, there is a learning action. Therefore it does not seem an improvement anymore to use RPROP.
